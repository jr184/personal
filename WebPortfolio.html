    The intent of this map is to display the user's choice of relief. I decided to take on a relief of Earth from a 'World from Space' projection, which obtains a planetary perspective from space. This particular take on Earth not only show cases the relief over Middle-East and Central Asia, but displays a what-if scenario of what Earth would be like if it has a Mars landmass cover.     Italy at Night

    Italy at Night

    This map is the winner of the GIS Day Map Gallery Competition of 2016 here at Humboldt State University, simulates Italy at night using the parcels of land that are provided by the National Land Cover Database.     Titan's Courtyard

    Titan's Courtyard

    This fantasy map was produced with an intent to complete as a midterm grade for the advance cartography course at Humboldt State. Stipulations were made which consist of breaking least one cartographic convention, but for good reason, the whole map must be relatively dense in terms of features and labels, demonstrate use of symbols and thematic unity, and an explanation as to what cartographic conventions were broken and why, an overview of the world you have mapped, and a description of techniques you used. The cartographic conventions that I decided to remove was a scale bar, a legend, and to have a land mass that is deceptively not an island. The land mass is supposed to be connected to more land than what is visually displayed. I decided not to incorporate the scale bar and legend, because the inspiration that I derived my map was from a game called Destiny. The original land mass outline was tranced over in adobe illustrator, where further work was conducted. Each section of the land mass has its own land feature, as mention in the names for each area region. The unity of the map was replicated from one of the maps in Destiny, specifically the Earth map. I wanted to follow the blue print idea of the map, where most of the elements have a drawn outline appearance, and as so, emulated the look with a pencil brush with a variety of strokes for each element. The compass rose and the other circular elements were replicated with multiple circles, and repeating lines. The lines were carefully rotated using the rotation tool centered on the initial circle. All the topographic patches were either a swatch or a symbol that were inspired by the USGS topographic symbols. Most of the layers exhibited some level of transparency to aid the focus of the land mass and its respective land features. I am no way affiliated with the developer of Bungie and the publisher of Activision. All copyrights of the original map are from Activision and Bungie.

  • The State of Jefferson

    The State of Jefferson has survived in the minds of this lands inhabitants as a "state of mind", encapsulating dreams of a fulfilled society. Because the State of Jefferson has never graced any official map, it has been undertaken by Connor Mullinix, a senior Geography major at Humboldt State, and myself to create an atlas of this long-standing, elusive dream as a introductory cartography project produced in Spring 2015. Though the spatial extent of Jefferson remains ephemeral, we have set out to map demographic, economic, social, and physical variables that unite this region currently riven by the borders of states which have incorporated these bucolic landscapes into their peripheries. The primary assumption was setting out to map something so elusive, is that there does exist a region of homogeneity that has precipitated from decades of shared experiences and most saliently, situations. As aforementioned, the area that can be broadly considered the State of Jefferson is split in two, whereby the northern half constitutes southern Oregon and its southern half forms northern California. This situation within the federal structure of the U.S. has aligned these lands towards the major urban conurbations of northern Oregon and southern California; allowing the overwhelming political force of these urban powerhouses to penetrate the rugged landscape. It is our aim to show the general population, unfamiliar with the State of Jefferson, how this shared predicament has come to shape the region. Aiming to evince this sense of a distinct region, we have set out to show the processes which are symptomatic of their shared situations. The mountainous topography has historically alienated the region from their respective states; however, the isolating quality of the mountains has begun attracting migrants as of late, beginning with the onset of "white flight" in the latter half of the 20th century, and continuing into the present. This influx of urban refugees and their money coincided with paradigmatic structural changes that greatly diminished the viability of primary industries - industries that have long sustained the people who call this place home. By showing the changing age structure of the region, and also mapping the development of economic factors, we hope to better understand how the history of this region has served to augment a sense for cessation and motivated thousands into once again calling for the autonomy of their own state - the State of Jefferson.

    The final product displays maps such as farm size over time, business composition & naming, age structure, unemployment, industry alignment, voting records, place of origin, and topography.

    Pacific Watershed Associates Telemeter Map

    Pacific Watershed Associates(PWA) contracted Justin Bissell, Brady Goodwin, and myself to generate an interactive map to display PWA's telemeter devices for their website. Each device displays data with variables consisting of temperature infared, flow total, flow rate, conductivity, and water temperature; with the user deciding their time selection such day, week, month, 6-month, and year. The project was completed in Fall of 2016.

    UNITED KINGDOM Crime 2015

    This particular map was generated in Mapbox Studio. The original intention was to manipulate font-sizes, font-types, colors of the roads, countries, etc. at various zoom levels.
